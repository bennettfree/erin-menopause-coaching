Trusted resources for this season of life

A curated collection of books, websites, podcasts, and tools I often share with women seeking clarity, context, and steady information during midlife.

Books section

Books I often recommend

These titles offer clear, thoughtful perspectives on menopause, midlife health, and whole-person wellbeing. They are resources I frequently share with women who want steady information and a deeper understanding of what they are experiencing.

The Menopause Guidebook

The Menopause Society (formerly NAMS), nonprofit medical society

I often recommend this to women who want clear, steady information without fear or hype. It helps you better understand what is happening in your body and feel more prepared for conversations with your healthcare provider.

The New Rules of Menopause

Stephanie S. Faubion, MD, MBA, medical director of The Menopause Society

I appreciate how practical and grounded this book feels. It offers education that respects individual experiences while helping women make sense of midlife changes.

The Menopause Manifesto

Jen Gunter, MD, OB GYN

A strong option for women who want facts and clarity. It challenges common myths and supports informed, thoughtful conversations about women’s health.

Estrogen Matters

Avrum Bluming, MD, oncologist and Carol Tavris, PhD, social psychologist

I suggest this for women who feel confused or uncertain about hormone conversations. It offers helpful historical and medical context without oversimplifying the topic.

The Menopause Brain

Lisa Mosconi, PhD, neuroscientist

This book is especially helpful for women noticing changes in memory, mood, or focus. I like how it connects brain health with everyday habits during midlife.

Burnout

Emily Nagoski, PhD, health educator and researcher

This is a powerful read for women feeling overwhelmed or depleted. It offers insight into stress, emotional health, and nervous system care in a very relatable way.

Websites Erin Recommends

The Menopause Society ↗

Purpose: menopause education and standards of care

A trusted medical organization offering clear, evidence-based information on menopause and midlife health. I recommend this site for women who want reliable education without trends, fear, or overwhelm.

Harvard Health Publishing ↗

Purpose: research-based lifestyle and health education

This site provides thoughtful articles on hormones, aging, nutrition, sleep, and stress. It’s a helpful resource for women who want deeper understanding grounded in medical research.

National Institute on Aging ↗

Purpose: aging and long-term health context

A government-backed resource focused on aging, midlife transitions, and preventive health. I like this site for understanding how menopause fits into overall health across the lifespan.

Office on Women’s Health ↗

Purpose: women’s health basics and preventive care

This U.S. Department of Health resource offers clear information on women’s health topics, including menopause, mental health, and wellness. It’s a solid reference for evidence-based guidance.

Podcasts Erin Recommends

The Dr. Louise Newson Podcast

Purpose: evidence-based menopause education

This podcast offers thoughtful conversations about menopause, hormones, and midlife health. It’s a good listen for women who want science-informed perspectives without sensationalism.

The Midlife Feast

Purpose: lived experience and normalization

A warm, honest podcast about midlife transitions, identity, health, and self-trust. I like it for helping women feel seen and less alone in this season.

On Health with Shannon Miller

Purpose: whole-body wellness and resilience

This podcast explores physical and emotional health through expert interviews and personal stories. It supports a balanced view of strength, recovery, and wellbeing in midlife.

Mobile Apps Erin Recommends

Balance App

Purpose: symptom tracking and education

A simple app that helps women track symptoms and patterns while offering educational content about menopause. Useful for building awareness and preparing for care conversations.

Insight Timer

Purpose: stress and nervous system support

This app offers guided meditations, breathwork, and sleep support. Many women find it helpful for managing stress, anxiety, and rest during midlife.

MyFitnessPal

Purpose: general nutrition awareness

A neutral tool for tracking food and habits without prescribing a specific diet. I suggest it only as a way to notice patterns, not to create restriction or pressure.
